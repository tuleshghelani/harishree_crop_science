Crops & applications

Glyphosate 41% SL fits burndown, directed, and non-crop programs. Typical patterns below - always follow your local label and agronomic advice.

  • Pre-plant burndown

    Prepare clean seedbeds by controlling existing vegetation before sowing or transplanting, per local label guidance.

  • Orchards & plantations

    Directed sprays along tree basins and inter-rows to manage weeds without contacting desirable bark or foliage.

  • Fallow & bund management

    Maintain firebreaks, bunds, and non-cropped strips where total vegetation control is required.

  • Non-crop areas

    Paths, farmyards, and industrial perimeters where labelled non-crop uses are permitted.

    Spot or strip applications to reduce weed competition along infrastructure—mind drift and buffer zones.

    Where permitted, supports uniform dry-down; always follow crop-specific pre-harvest intervals.

Technical specifications

Product information

Brand:Harishree Crop Science

Formulation:Soluble liquid (SL)
Mode of action:Group G / HRAC 9 — EPSP synthase inhibitor (systemic)
Shelf life:Use within shelf life printed on the pack; store sealed

Packs & application

Pack sizes:500 ml, 1 L, 5 L, 20 L — available through Harishree dealers
Recommended dose (knapsack):150-200 ml per 16-20 litre pump load (adjust to weed stage and water volume as directed)
Spray quality:Medium to coarse droplets; avoid drift to desirable plants

Mode of action

Systemic movement and EPSP synthase inhibition (HRAC Group 9)

  1. 1

    Foliar uptake

    The solution is retained on leaf surfaces and absorbed into living green tissue for movement through the plant.

  2. 2

    Translocation

    Glyphosate moves with assimilates to growing points, including roots and underground storage organs on susceptible species.

  3. 3

    EPSP synthase inhibition

    Disrupts aromatic amino acid biosynthesis, leading to gradual yellowing and collapse of treated weeds over several days.

  4. 4

    Complete burn-down

    Susceptible weeds stop competing with crops or clear the area for replanting once degradation aligns with your program.

Usage & safety guidelines

Glyphosate products require respect for drift, PPE, and non-selective activity

Application discipline Worker and crop safety

Usage best practices

Apply accurately for clean control and consistent field performance.

  • 1

    Personal protection

    Wear full-length clothing, chemical-resistant gloves, and eye protection. Use a face shield or mist mask if drift is possible.

  • 2

    Non-selective hazard

    H ROUND kills green plants it contacts. Shield desirable crops and ornamentals from spray drift.

  • 3

    Mixing order

    Half-fill the tank with clean water, add product as directed, agitate, then top up. Rinse measuring tools safely.

Safety and stewardship

Protect people, desirable crops, and the surrounding environment.

  • 4

    Storage

    Keep the original container closed, away from food, feed, and children. Store in a cool, dry, locked area.

  • 5

    Disposal

    Triple-rinse empties; dispose of rinsate and containers per local regulations.

  • 6

    Environmental care

    Avoid spray near water bodies unless permitted; respect label buffer distances.

Frequently asked questions

H ROUND is a non-selective systemic herbicide for controlling many annual and perennial weeds and unwanted vegetation in approved burndown, directed, and non-crop uses. Always read and follow the product label and local regulations.

For typical knapsack applications, use 150-200 ml per 16-20 litre pump of spray solution unless your agronomist recommends a different rate for weed size and water volume.

Glyphosate is non-selective: it can injure any green plant it contacts. Use directed sprays, shields, or pre-plant timings to protect crops. Never spray over the top of desirable vegetation unless the label explicitly allows it for that crop.

Contact herbicides mainly burn foliage. H ROUND moves systemically in susceptible weeds, improving control of perennials with strong root systems when coverage is good and weeds are actively growing.
