Mode of action

Pendimethalin acts rapidly during seed germination to halt cell division, starving the emerging weed.

  1. 1

    Root & Shoot Absorption

    Pendimethalin 30% EC is absorbed primarily by the roots and emerging shoots of germinating weeds.

  2. 2

    Microtubule Inhibition

    It acts by inhibiting microtubule assembly, which is essential for cell division and cell elongation.

  3. 3

    Growth Arrest

    Weed seedlings fail to emerge or die shortly after emergence due to halted root and shoot growth.

  4. 4

    Residual Protection

    Forms a protective chemical barrier in the top soil layer, preventing new weed flushes for an extended period.
